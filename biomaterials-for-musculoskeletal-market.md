<p><p>The musculoskeletal market encompasses three primary types of biomaterials: </p><p>1. **Metallic Biomaterials** are typically used in load-bearing applications due to their high strength and durability, often employed in implants like orthopedic screws and plates. </p><p>2. **Polymeric Biomaterials** offer versatility and flexibility, making them suitable for scaffolds and drug delivery systems. </p><p>3. **Inorganic Non-Metallic Biomaterials** include ceramics and bioactive glasses, ideal for bone replacement and repair, as they promote osseointegration and mimic natural bone properties. Together, these materials enhance musculoskeletal treatments and surgical outcomes.</p></p>
