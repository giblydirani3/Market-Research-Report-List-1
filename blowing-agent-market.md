<p><p>The blowing agent market is categorized into several types based on chemical composition. HCFC (Hydrochlorofluorocarbon) is often used for foam production but faces regulatory restrictions due to its ozone-depleting potential. HFC (Hydrofluorocarbon) is a commonly used alternative, although it has high global warming potential. HC (Hydrocarbon) blowing agents, like pentane and isobutane, are environmentally friendly but flammable. Other categories may include CO2 and water-based agents, which are gaining popularity for their lower environmental impact and regulatory compliance.</p></p>
